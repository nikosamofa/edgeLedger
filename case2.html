Client B :

Decreased Drop-offs By 86%

Client B (NDA Protected)

Challenge 

Client B is a UK-based marketplace for construction material manufacturers and customers.

The co-founders wanted to optimize their website experience by improving three problem areas:

  • Poorly placed calls to action (CTAs), where mobile users could not see the CTA clearly enough to click on it
  • Customer drop-offs at the checkout step because users only wanted to check the delivery time
  • A complicated cookie policy that caused visitors to bounce as soon as they landed on the website

Action plan

  • Heuristic analysis - asked expert users to perform the key tasks within the conversion funnel (e.g. login, find product X, add product X to cart) to discover user pain points and report
  • Focus groups to discuss needs and feelings towards the product after direct observation of one user at a time - 6 users discussed issues and concerns about features on the website
  • Used Hotjar Recordings and Heatmaps to dig deeper into these UX research issues:
    1. We improved the visibility of CTAs with changes to font, color, and design. The team also included a rating widget next to the CTAs to display social proof.
    2. We removed the need to set up an account to place an order and added an estimated delivery date for every product
    3. We implemented design changes in the cookie policy pop-up to make it 30% bigger with better color and copy. They also placed the banner on the top of the page.

The result was a gradual upgrade of the website to meet—and exceed—customer expectations.

Results

 
  • Redesigning the checkout flow decreased drop-offs by 86%.
  • The conversion rate tripled to 1.6% — massively boosting yearly revenue by more than £10,000.

Key Takeaways

  • UX research tools like recordings are a great way to understand user behavior on your website—you can sort and filter recordings by relevance
